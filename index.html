I am a Ph.D. candidate at Johns Hopkins University working in the Flow Physics and Computation Laboratory with Prof. Rajat Mittal.

At Hopkins, I primarily focus on developing mathematical and numerical models, with a strong emphasis on software engineering and AI/ML optimized for high-performance computing platforms in computational mechanics. This interdisciplinary endeavor involves the integration of various domains, including computer science, applied mathematics, machine learning, computational mechanics, and theoretical fluid dynamics.

A typical day for me involves translating the mathematical models that govern the aeroelastic physics of highly deformable bio-inspired wings into a high-fidelity computer code. This requires a lot of debugging and optimization to ensure that the code runs extremely fast on thousands of processors to produce simulation results. Once the simulation is complete, I work on post-processing the large datasets, which can easily consist of 200+ million data points for each timestep (I can have hundreds of timesteps in one simulation), each with at least 4 degrees of freedom. Analyzing this data and extracting meaningful engineering insights is an extremely complex task, and that's where I put my machine learning skills to work.
