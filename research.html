Research Work

My research interests lie in the area of Fluid Dynamics and specifically in Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD). I have never restricted myself to one area but have continuously looked for opportunities to increase my perspective of the domain. Over my journey, I studied not just incompressible flows but also compressible flows. Apart from this, I've simulated flows at the macro scale as well as mesoscale.

My interests are as follows:
  • Aerodynamics

    It is one of the application areas of my research. I am particularly interested in developing new numerical tools to analyze airflow around bodies and studying the various quantities of interest. I am also interested in exploring the physics of phenomena like flow separation, etc., and devise techniques to reduce them. It will lead to reduced drag and eventually resulting in lower flight operating costs. Previously, I have intensively worked on aerodynamic shape optimization of multi-element airfoils using a special class of genetic algorithms called Invasive Weed Optimisation.

  • Computational Fluid Dynamics

    Computational Fluid Dynamics is the backbone of all my investigations. I am excited by the whole idea of defining a real physical system within a virtual environment and then incorporating physical law (governing equations) to study the spatial and temporal evolution of the system. I have experience in working with Reynolds Averaged Navier Stokes (RANS) based simulations and Large Eddy Simulations (LES). Previously, I have used these techniques to study flow control, separated flows, multiphase flows, etc. Further, I am interested in developing novel algorithms to simulate fluid flows efficiently.

  • Data Driven Modeling

    In this age, the data is available from various sources like research papers, databases, etc. This data can be used to develop low dimensional and efficient surrogate models, reduced-order models, etc. to resolve the bottlenecks and challenges faced in existing CFD algorithms. Such bottlenecks not just become a massive source of increased expenditures but also increases the time required in developing a new design where computationally expensive simulations are to run again and again. I have good experience in using Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) like Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) to develop surrogate models, both black box and physics informed. In the future, I wanted to explore avenues to use data-driven techniques in conjunction with conventional numerical methods to reduce the computational expenses of performing simulations. I also wanted to work in areas where data-driven techniques can be used in turbulence closure modeling, reduced-order modeling, Multidisciplinary design optimization, etc.
